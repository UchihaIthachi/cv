# Profile Structure Reasoning

This document outlines the specific reasoning behind the section ordering chosen for each targeted CV/Resume profile in this repository.

## General Principles
All profiles start with the standard header, followed by a `\MainDivider` (in the multi-page CVs) to ensure a clean visual separation. Next comes the **Summary**, **Education**, and **Experience**. These foundational sections remain consistent in their ordering because hiring managers and ATS systems generally expect this flow.

The variation occurs primarily in the placement of the **Projects** and **Certificates** sections, which are adjusted based on the role's specific needs and hiring culture.

---

### DevOps Profile
**Order:** Experience -> **Certificates** -> **Projects** -> Skills
- **Reasoning:** In Cloud, Infrastructure, DevOps, and SRE roles, industry-recognized certifications (like the CKA, AWS Solutions Architect, and specific Bootcamps) serve as hard filters for recruiters. They establish immediate baseline trust in a candidate's ability to safely operate production infrastructure. Placing these directly after experience ensures they are seen immediately, followed by the practical application of those skills in the Projects section.

### Backend and Fullstack Profiles
**Order:** Experience -> **Projects** -> **Certificates** -> Skills
- **Reasoning:** Software Engineering roles typically prioritize demonstrated building capability over formal certifications. Hiring managers want to see *what* you have built, the architecture decisions you've made, and the impact of the software. Therefore, the Projects section takes precedence over Certificates.

### Web3 Profile
**Order:** Experience -> **Projects** -> **Certificates** -> Skills
- **Reasoning:** The Web3 industry is notoriously portfolio-driven. Open-source contributions, live dApps, deployed smart contracts, and theoretical knowledge (like ZK-rollups) are the primary currency of a Web3 developer. Placing Projects before Certificates ensures that Github links, architecture diagrams, and live demos are front-and-center.

### General Profile
**Order:** Experience -> **Projects** -> **Articles** -> **Certificates** -> Skills
- **Reasoning:** The General profile serves as a comprehensive overview of all capabilities. It follows the standard Software Engineering flow (Projects before Certs) but also includes a unique "Articles" section to highlight technical writing and communication skills, which are universally valuable.
