2.  **Overly Long Summary:** The summary is a dense paragraph of buzzwords. It should be a concise, 2-3 line statement of the candidate's skills and career goals.
3.  **"Keyword Stuffing" in Skills Section:** The skills section is just a long list of technologies. It would be better to group them by proficiency (e.g., "Expert," "Proficient," "Familiar").
4.  **Vague Internship Bullets:** Some of the internship bullets are good, but others are a bit vague and could be strengthened with more specific metrics.
5.  **Too Many Projects:** Five projects is a lot for a one-page resume. It's better to have 3-4 strong projects with detailed, results-oriented descriptions than 5 weaker ones.
6.  **Minor Formatting Inconsistencies:** There are some minor inconsistencies in spacing and formatting that could be cleaned up.
7.  **Generic "High School" Entry:** The high school entry is unnecessary and takes up valuable space.
8.  **No "soft skills" mentioned:** While the focus should be on technical skills, mentioning things like teamwork, communication, and problem-solving in the context of projects can be beneficial.

## 6. Formatting & Length

*   **Order of Sections:** Header, Summary, Education, Experience, Projects, Skills. (Current order is good).
*   **Fonts:** Use a clean, professional sans-serif font like Calibri, Arial, or Helvetica.
*   **Margins:** 0.5-1 inch margins on all sides.
*   **One vs. Two Pages:** Stick to one page. This is a hard rule for new grads.
*   **File Name:** `FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf` (e.g., `Harshana_Fernando_Resume.pdf`).
*   **PDF vs. DOCX:** Always submit as a PDF.
